# Dev Edition Status Bar

## Stormy Linux Dev Status Bar

The status bar used in **Stormy Linux Developer Edition** is the default Qtile status bar. Built in widgets are what is being leveraged in combination with the JetBrainsMono Nerd Font to display the icons. All system processes that are being used in the status bar are default widgets pulled from qtile.

> Note: Qtile Extras are being used in a pop-up menu with three main purposes, **logout**, **reboot** and **shutdown**.

## The Menu

The default applications menu for **Stormy Linux** is **Rofi**. By clicking the **Stormy Linux Logo** at the top left corner in the status bar, the **Rofi** menu will pop up in the middle of your screen where you can access all your applications and more.

## Workspace Icons

Workspace icons are all hidden by default, except for the **"Home"** icon, other workspace can easily be navigated to with a simple key combination. There are a total of **16** available workspaces for you to leverage, and each workspace has a purpose, see the list below.

All workspaces are displayed on the top left corner of the status bar, right next to the **Stormy Linux** logo.

- Workspace 1: **Home** (Optional workspace)
- Workspace 2: **VIM** (Programming text editor)
- Workspace 3: **VSCode** (A feature-rich IDE)
- Workspace 4: **Browser**(Firefox, Brave, Chrome, etc.)
- Workspace 5: **Development Server**(Go, Python, NPM, PNPM, BUN server, etc)
- Workspace 6: **Testing and Debugging**(Cypress, Chai, Jest, etc)
- Workspace 7: **GitHub**(WebUI or GitHub-Desktop)
- Workspace 8: **UI/UX**(GIMP, Figma, Lunacy, etc.)
- Workspace 9: **All purpose terminal**(Kitty, Terminator, Alacritty, Tilix, etch.)
- Workspace 10: **File Explorers**(Krusader, Caja, Thunar, Ranger, etc.)
- Workspace 11: **Notes**(Notion, Obsidian, etc.)
- Workspace 12: **EMail**(Protonmail, Thunderbird, GMail, etc.)
- Workspace 13: **Spotify** (Spotify App for your favorite music)
- Workspace 14: **VLC** (For Videos or Music)
- Workspace 15: **Settings**(system settings, config file, etc.)
- Workspace 16: **Games** (PC games of your choosing)

## Workspace Colors

**Active**

Active workspaces have a green highlighted icon when an application is active in that workspace, when you select and navigate to that workspace it will have an active "rose" like pink-ish foreground color and an active bottom border with a blue accent color.

**Inactive**

These are the workspaces with no activity, these are the icons that are hidden, and can be accessed by navigating to them.
